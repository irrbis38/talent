Your People Strategy in a Post-COVID-19 World

True or false: There is no “going back to the way it used to be” after the “COVID-19 dust” settles.

True. Employees are proving that they can get work done anywhere despite kids screaming for homework help, dogs barking, and bored teenagers perfecting TikTok dances. From this point forward, every organization will have some form of remote work policy.

True or false: Work from home policies will impact how an organization grows and evolves.

True. One of the primary ways businesses grow and evolve is through the people they hire. A defined remote work policy will allow organizations to adopt a “talent anywhere” approach and ensure they hire the best and brightest (as well as propel the company forward).

True or false: Now is the perfect time to assess your people strategy.

True. I’ve talked with several business leaders, and they all agree that now is the perfect time to prepare for the future. So many people are unemployed and looking (or will be) for jobs that you need to be ready. Ask yourself these questions to help you assess your people strategy:

  • Who are the top five A-players that instantly come to mind? What skills and competencies do they possess that make them A-players?
  • Does your top talent know they’re considered A-players? Do they understand the impact they would have on the organization if they left?
  • How can you engage and utilize your A-players to help lead your organization post-COVID-19?
  • Having to quickly adjust to accommodate a changing work world often makes organizational gaps more apparent or pressing. What are your most significant areas of opportunity? How do you plan to address them in the short- and long-term?
  • Which departments have the highest client turnover? Employee turnover?
  • What’s keeping you up at night?

If you are a senior leader, I highly recommend doing a talent inventory. It would help if you had A+ players leading your people to be successful post-COVID-19.
